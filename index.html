The Ultimate Chocolate Chip Cookies

Prep Time: 15 minutes
Bake Time: 12 minutes
Total Time: 27 minutes

Why These Cookies Are Amazing

These chocolate chip cookies are absolutely delicious and perfect for any occasion. They're crispy on the outside, soft and chewy on the inside, and packed with chocolate chips!

Ingredients

Dry Ingredients:

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
    • You can use regular or whole wheat
    • Make sure it's fresh (not expired)
  • 1 teaspoon baking soda
  • 1 teaspoon salt
    • Sea salt works best
    • Regular table salt is also fine
  • 2 cups chocolate chips
    • Semi-sweet is traditional
    • Dark chocolate is also delicious
    • Milk chocolate for sweeter cookies

Wet Ingredients:

  • 1 cup butter (softened)
    • Leave at room temperature for 30 minutes
    • Should be soft but not melted
  • 3/4 cup white sugar
  • 3/4 cup brown sugar
    • Light brown or dark brown both work
  • 2 eggs
    • Large eggs are best
    • Room temperature eggs mix better
  • 2 teaspoons vanilla extract

Instructions

  1. Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C)
    • Make sure the oven rack is in the middle position
    • Remove any other items from the oven
  2. Mix the butter and both sugars together until creamy and smooth
    • This should take about 3-4 minutes
    • Use an electric mixer if you have one
  3. Add eggs and vanilla extract to the butter mixture
    • Add one egg at a time
    • Mix well after each egg
  4. In a separate bowl, combine:
    • Flour
      • Measure carefully
      • Spoon it into the measuring cup
    • Baking soda
    • Salt
      • Mix these dry ingredients together first
  5. Slowly add the dry ingredients to the wet ingredients
    • Add a little at a time
    • Mix on low speed
    • Stop mixing when just combined
  6. Fold in the chocolate chips
    • Use a spoon or spatula
    • Don't overmix
  7. Drop spoonfuls of dough onto a greased baking sheet
    • Leave 2 inches between each cookie
    • Use a cookie scoop for uniform size
  8. Bake for 10-12 minutes or until golden brown
    • Watch carefully during the last 2 minutes
    • Edges should be golden
    • Centers may look slightly underdone
  9. Let cool for 5 minutes before eating
    • Use a spatula to transfer to a cooling rack
    • Be careful - they're hot!

Fun Facts About Chocolate Chip Cookies

  • The chocolate chip cookie was invented in 1938 by Ruth Wakefield
    • She owned the Toll House Inn in Massachusetts
    • She expected the chocolate to melt completely
    • Instead, the chocolate chips held their shape!
  • Americans eat over 7 billion chocolate chip cookies per year!
    • That's about 21 cookies per person
    • The average American family makes cookies 3 times per month
  • The chemical formula for chocolate is C7H8N4O2
    • This is actually the formula for theobromine
    • Theobromine is the main compound in chocolate
  • The world's largest chocolate chip cookie weighed over 40,000 pounds
    • It was made in 2003
    • It was 102 feet wide
    • It used 30,000 eggs!

Pro Tips

  1. Don't overmix the dough - this makes cookies tough
    • Mix until ingredients are just combined
    • Overmixing develops gluten
  2. Use room temperature ingredients for best results
    • Butter should be soft
    • Eggs should not be cold from the fridge
  3. Try these variations:
    • Add 1 cup of chopped nuts
      • Walnuts are classic
      • Pecans add a buttery flavor
    • Mix in 1/2 cup of peanut butter chips
      • Combine with chocolate chips
      • Or use instead of chocolate chips
    • Sprinkle sea salt on top before baking
      • Use flaky sea salt
      • Just a small pinch on each cookie
  4. Cookies continue to cook after you remove them from the oven
    • They will firm up as they cool
    • Don't overbake or they'll be too hard
  5. Store in an airtight container for up to 1 week
    • Layer with parchment paper
    • Keep at room temperature
    • Don't refrigerate - this makes them dry

Temperature Conversion

375°F = 190°C = 463K
(K stands for Kelvin, the scientific temperature scale)

Formula: °C = (°F - 32) × 5/9

Mathematical Note:

If the recipe makes 48 cookies and you want to make only 24 cookies, you need to multiply each ingredient by 1/2 or 0.5

Example: 2 cups flour × 1/2 = 1 cup flour
